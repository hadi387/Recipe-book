Chin Chin Recipe

A delightful recipe for making Nigerian chin chin, a crunchy and sweet fried pastry.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the all-purpose flour, granulated sugar, salt, and baking powder.
  2. Add the softened unsalted butter to the dry ingredients and mix until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the egg, then add the milk and vanilla extract. Mix well.
  4. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until a dough forms. Knead the dough gently until it's smooth and elastic.
  5. Divide the dough into smaller portions and roll each portion into a thin sheet about 1/4 inch thick.
  6. Using a knife or a pizza cutter, cut the rolled dough into small squares or rectangles.
  7. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or a deep pot to 350°F (175°C).
  8. Carefully drop the cut dough into the hot oil and fry until golden brown and crispy, stirring occasionally to ensure even frying.
  9. Remove the fried chin chin from the oil using a slotted spoon and drain them on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  10. Allow the chin chin to cool completely before serving. They will become crunchier as they cool.
  11. Store the cooled chin chin in an airtight container for up to two weeks.

Enjoy your delicious homemade chin chin!
