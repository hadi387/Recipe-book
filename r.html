Jollof Rice

A flavorful and vibrant one-pot dish, popular in Nigeria and across West Africa.

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium-sized tomatoes, blended
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 teaspoon ground paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 green bellpepper
  • 1 teaspoon dried or fresh parsley
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Vegetable oil for frying

    • Instructions:

    1. Heat vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add chopped onions, minced garlic, and diced bell peppers. Saut√© until softened.
    2. Add blended tomatoes, ginger, paprika, thyme, curry powder, parsley, salt, and pepper. Stir well and cook until the mixture thickens and the oil separates.
    3. Wash the parboiled rice and drain. Add the rice to the pot and stir to coat it with the tomato mixture.
    4. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth and tomato sauce. Stir to combine.
    5. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for about 20-25 minutes or until the rice is cooked and all the liquid is absorbed. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
    6. Once cooked, fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot.

    Enjoy your delicious Jollof Rice with your favorite side dishes or protein options!
